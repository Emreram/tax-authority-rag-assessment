================================================================================
HyDE PROMPT — Hypothetical Document Embedding
================================================================================

PURPOSE:
  Generate a hypothetical legal passage that WOULD answer the user's question,
  then use its embedding for retrieval instead of the raw question embedding.
  This bridges the vocabulary gap between casual user questions and formal
  legal document language.

WHEN USED:
  Only for SIMPLE conceptual queries WITHOUT legal references.
  Decision gate in: module3_crag_statemachine.py → classify_query() node

  USE when:
    - Query is conceptual without specific legal terminology
    - User doesn't know the relevant article numbers or law names
    - Example: "Can I deduct my home office expenses?"

  DO NOT USE when:
    - Query contains ECLI references (exact-ID path is better)
    - Query contains Article/Artikel references (already precise)
    - Query is a retry attempt (HyDE already tried, don't double-apply)
    - Query is COMPLEX (decomposition is used instead)

  Latency cost: ~300-500ms (LLM generation + embedding)
  Only applied when the benefit outweighs the latency penalty.

DESIGN NOTES:
  - "accuracy of specific numbers is less important than using the right legal
    terminology" — HyDE doesn't need to be factually correct. Its purpose is to
    generate text that SOUNDS like a real legal passage, so the embedding is
    closer to actual legal documents in vector space.
  - Dutch output when the question is Dutch — the embedding model is multilingual
    but performs best when query and passage are in the same language.
  - The hypothetical text is used ONLY for embedding/retrieval. The actual answer
    is generated from REAL retrieved passages, never from the HyDE text.

================================================================================

--- SYSTEM PROMPT ---

You are a senior Dutch tax law expert. Given a tax question, write a short
paragraph (3-5 sentences) that would appear in an official legal text or policy
document answering this question. Include specific article numbers, legal terms,
and provisions if you can infer them. Write in Dutch if the question is in Dutch.

IMPORTANT: This is a hypothetical answer used for retrieval — accuracy of specific
numbers is less important than using the right legal terminology and structure.


--- USER PROMPT ---

{query}


--- EXAMPLE INPUT/OUTPUT ---

Input:  "Can I deduct my home office expenses?"

Output: "Op grond van artikel 3.17 Wet IB 2001 zijn kosten voor een werkruimte
         in de eigen woning aftrekbaar indien de werkruimte een naar verkeersopvatting
         zelfstandig gedeelte van de woning vormt en de belastingplichtige in die
         werkruimte ten minste 70% van zijn inkomen verdient. De aftrekbaarheid
         is beperkt tot de werkelijk gemaakte kosten verminderd met het
         huurwaardeforfait."

Why this works:
  The raw question "Can I deduct my home office expenses?" contains no Dutch
  legal terms. Its embedding would be far from the actual legal passages.

  The HyDE text contains: "artikel 3.17", "Wet IB 2001", "werkruimte",
  "eigen woning", "aftrekbaar", "huurwaardeforfait" — all terms that appear
  in the real legal text. Its embedding is much closer to the actual article
  in vector space, improving retrieval recall.

Input:  "Wat zijn de regels voor schenking aan kinderen?"

Output: "Ingevolge artikel 33 van de Successiewet 1956 geldt voor schenkingen
         van ouders aan kinderen een jaarlijkse vrijstelling. Voor het
         kalenderjaar 2024 bedraagt de reguliere vrijstelling 6.035 euro per
         kind. Daarnaast bestaat op grond van artikel 33a een eenmalig verhoogde
         vrijstelling van 28.947 euro voor kinderen tussen 18 en 40 jaar."
